Cauliflower Soup

Ingredients

- cauliflower - medium carrots - chicken breasts - chicken stock - eggs - fresh lemon juice - salt and pepper - eggs - 1 tsp of salt - 4oz plain flour

Directions

1.Clean the cauliflower and divide it into flourettes, cut the stem into small cubes. 2.Cut the cleaned carrots into rounds and chop the chicken breast into cubes. 3.Bring the chicken stock to a simmer. 4.Add the cauliflower and the carrots. 5.Bring to a boil, cook for about 5 minutes, then add the chicken cubes. 6.In the meantime make the dumplings. 7.Separate the 2 eggs needed for the dumplings and give the egg whites to a smallish bowl. 8.Whisk the egg whites and the salt with a fork until frothy. 9.Add the lightly beaten egg yolks, incorporate, then carefully fold in the flour. The batter should be pretty soft. 10.Dip a teaspoon into the soup and use it to give the dumplings to the soup. 11.Cut out small amounts of batter with it (the teaspoon should be about Â¾ full with batter) and let the batter slide into the soup. 12.Simmer the dumplings for about 10 minutes or until soft. 13.The pot should be covered with the lid, but leave a crack open. 14.In the meantime beat the 3 eggs for the soup with the freshly squeezed lemon juice. 15.When the dumplings are cooked, take one ladleful of soup and pour it slowly and carefully over the eggs while stirring all the time. 16.Repeat with another ladleful of soup. 17.Take the soup off the heat and slowly whisk in the egg mixture. 18.Don't cook the soup anymore. 19.Adjust the soup with salt and pepper and serve immediately. 20.If you wish to reheat the soup the next day, you can do so, but take care that the soup doesn't come to a boil, the eggs might scramble then and the soup will not be creamy anymore, it will still taste good though.
